## Act overview

**Full title:** Privacy Act 2020
**Commencement:** 1 December 2020 (except IPP3A — see below)
**Administered by:** Office of the Privacy Commissioner (OPC)
**Applies to:** Any agency (person or body) that holds personal information about individuals in New Zealand. Individuals acting in a personal, family, or household capacity are not agencies.

---

## Information Privacy Principles (IPPs) — summary

The Privacy Act 2020 contains 13 Information Privacy Principles (IPP 1–13) plus IPP3A (effective 1 May 2026).

### IPP 1 — Purpose of collection

An agency may only collect personal information if:
- It is collected for a lawful purpose connected with the agency's activities.
- The collection is necessary for that purpose.

**What a gap looks like:** Collecting employee contact details "just in case" with no specified purpose. Collecting customer data for a purpose that was never disclosed to the individual.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP1`

---

### IPP 2 — Source of personal information

Where an agency collects personal information about an individual, the agency must collect it directly from that individual unless:
- The individual authorises collection from another source.
- Another exemption applies (law enforcement, publicly available, etc.).

**What a gap looks like:** Collecting personal information from a third party (e.g. a recruitment agency or credit bureau) without authorisation or an applicable exemption.

**Note:** IPP2 relates to *where* the agency collects from. IPP3A (effective 1 May 2026) adds a notice obligation when indirect collection occurs.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP2`

---

### IPP 3 — Collection of personal information from individual

When an agency collects personal information directly from the individual, it must take reasonable steps to ensure the individual knows:
- The fact that the information is being collected.
- The purpose for which it is collected.
- The agency's identity and how to contact them.
- Whether collection is voluntary or mandatory, and the consequences of not providing it.
- The individual's right to access and correct the information.

**What a gap looks like:** No privacy disclosure at point of collection (e.g. no privacy policy on a website form, no verbal disclosure at time of service intake).

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP3`

---

### IPP 3A — Collection from other sources (indirect collection)

**Effective: 1 May 2026.** See full detail in `ipp-3a.md`.

When an agency collects personal information about an individual **from a source other than the individual**, the agency must take reasonable steps to notify the individual of the collection, purpose, agency identity, and access/correction rights.

**What a gap looks like:** Employer receives employee data from a rostering app that feeds an overseas payroll processor, without employees being notified of the indirect data flow.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s22A (IPP3A)`

---

### IPP 4 — Manner of collection

Personal information must not be collected by unlawful means, or by means that are unfair or that intrude unduly on the personal affairs of the individual.

**What a gap looks like:** Covert surveillance, misleading consent forms, or collecting personal data from an individual without their knowledge in a context where they would reasonably expect privacy.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP4`

---

### IPP 5 — Storage and security

An agency must ensure that personal information it holds is:
- Protected by security safeguards that are reasonable in the circumstances.
- Protected against loss, access, use, modification, or disclosure that is not authorised.
- Disposed of securely when no longer needed.

**What a gap looks like:** No data processing agreement with a cloud provider that holds customer data. Employee records stored in unencrypted spreadsheets. No process for secure disposal of physical records.

**Key question for vendor register:** Does a data processing agreement (DPA) exist with each third-party holding personal information on the agency's behalf?

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP5`

---

### IPP 6 — Access to personal information

Individuals have the right to request access to personal information that an agency holds about them. The agency must provide that information within 20 working days (subject to limited exceptions).

**What a gap looks like:** No process for handling access requests. No person responsible for privacy requests. No documented response procedure.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP6`

---

### IPP 7 — Correction of personal information

Where personal information held by an agency is incorrect, the agency must, if requested by the individual, correct it or attach a statement of the correction sought.

**What a gap looks like:** No process for handling correction requests. Correction requests routed to customer service without a defined outcome. No documented correction procedure.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP7`

---

### IPP 8 — Accuracy

Before using or disclosing personal information, an agency must take reasonable steps to ensure it is accurate, up to date, complete, relevant, and not misleading.

**What a gap looks like:** Using an outdated customer address list for marketing. Using historical employment data for a reference check without verifying currency.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP8`

---

### IPP 9 — Retention

An agency must not keep personal information for longer than is necessary for the purpose for which it may lawfully be used.

**What a gap looks like:** No data retention policy. Employee records retained indefinitely. Customer data never deleted after the relationship ends. No scheduled review of held data.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP9`

---

### IPP 10 — Limits on use

Personal information obtained for one purpose must not be used for any other purpose, unless one of the limited exceptions applies (individual authorises it, the use is directly related to the original purpose, or other specific statutory grounds).

**What a gap looks like:** Using a customer email list (collected for service delivery) for unsolicited marketing without consent. Using employment records for purposes unrelated to the employment relationship.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP10`

---

### IPP 11 — Limits on disclosure

An agency must not disclose personal information unless the disclosure is one of the purposes for which it was collected, is directly related to that purpose, the individual authorises it, or another specific exception applies.

**What a gap looks like:** Sharing customer records with a third party without consent or a lawful basis. Disclosing an employee's personal details to another employer without authorisation.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP11`

---

### IPP 12 — Unique identifiers

An agency must not assign unique identifiers to individuals unless necessary. It must not require individuals to disclose unique identifiers assigned by another agency unless certain conditions are met.

**What a gap looks like:** Collecting IRD numbers for purposes that do not require them. Requiring customers to provide a driver licence number when a non-identifying alternative would work.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP12`

---

### IPP 13 — Transborder data flows

An agency in New Zealand must not disclose personal information to a foreign person or entity unless the agency believes the recipient is subject to laws that provide comparable safeguards, the individual authorises disclosure, or another specific exception applies.

**What a gap looks like:** Sending customer data to an overseas cloud provider without assessing whether the destination country has comparable privacy protections. Using a US-hosted CRM without reviewing data transfer compliance.

**Citation format:** `Privacy Act 2020 s21 IPP13`

---

## Notifiable privacy breaches (Part 6)

An agency must notify the Privacy Commissioner and affected individuals of a privacy breach that:
- Involves personal information it holds, and
- Has caused, or is likely to cause, **serious harm** to an affected individual.

**Serious harm factors** include the sensitivity of the information, the number of individuals affected, the likelihood of misuse, and the vulnerability of the affected individuals.

**Notification timeline:** As soon as practicable after the agency becomes aware.

**What a gap looks like:** No documented breach response procedure. No one responsible for assessing whether a breach is notifiable. No record of past breaches assessed.

## Key definitions

- **Personal information:** Information about an identifiable individual (including information that can be linked with other information to identify a person).
- **Agency:** Any person or body that holds personal information (with exceptions for individuals acting in a personal or household capacity, and small businesses holding personal information about fewer than 20 individuals in certain limited circumstances).
- **Interference with privacy:** A breach of one of the IPPs that causes, or is likely to cause, harm to an individual.
- **Harm:** Includes financial loss, damage to reputation, humiliation, harassment, physical harm, and loss of employment or business opportunities.
